Explore the Frozen Tundra

Step into the harsh yet beautiful world of the Arctic tundra and discover the incredible animals that have adapted to survive in one of Earth's most extreme environments. From the powerful polar bear to the clever Arctic fox, each species has developed unique strategies to thrive in the frozen wilderness. Learn about their remarkable adaptations and the challenges they face in our changing climate.

Arctic apex predator

Polar Bear

The largest land carnivore on Earth, perfectly adapted to the Arctic environment with thick fur and powerful swimming abilities.

Key Facts:

  • Weight: 330-1,500 lbs
  • Height: 4-5 feet at shoulder
  • Diet: Seals, fish
  • Status: Vulnerable

Camouflage master

Arctic Fox

Small but resilient predators with seasonal color changes, perfectly adapted to the extreme Arctic conditions.

Key Facts:

  • Weight: 6-17 lbs
  • Fur: Changes color seasonally
  • Diet: Lemmings, birds, eggs
  • Status: Least Concern

Pack hunters

Arctic Wolf

Powerful pack hunters with thick white fur, essential predators in the Arctic ecosystem.

Key Facts:

  • Weight: 70-180 lbs
  • Pack size: 5-20 wolves
  • Diet: Muskoxen, caribou
  • Status: Least Concern

Ancient survivors

Muskox

Ancient survivors from the Ice Age, these massive herbivores have thick coats and defensive formations.

Key Facts:

  • Weight: 400-900 lbs
  • Height: 4-5 feet
  • Diet: Grasses, sedges
  • Status: Least Concern

Long-distance migrators

Caribou

Majestic migratory animals that undertake the longest land migration of any mammal on Earth.

Key Facts:

  • Weight: 200-700 lbs
  • Migration: Up to 3,000 miles
  • Diet: Lichens, grasses
  • Status: Vulnerable

Silent hunters

Snowy Owl

Magnificent white owls perfectly adapted to Arctic conditions, known for their silent flight and keen hunting skills.

Key Facts:

  • Wingspan: 4-5 feet
  • Weight: 3-6 lbs
  • Diet: Lemmings, small mammals
  • Status: Least Concern
