Explore the Vibrant Tropical Aviary

Step into our lush tropical aviary and discover the most colorful and fascinating birds from rainforests around the world. From brilliant parrots to majestic toucans, each species showcases the incredible diversity and beauty of tropical birdlife. Learn about their unique adaptations, behaviors, and the vital role they play in their ecosystems.

Brilliant colors

Scarlet Macaw

One of the most recognizable parrots with vibrant red, blue, and yellow plumage, known for their intelligence and long lifespan.

Key Facts:

  • Lifespan: 40-50 years
  • Length: 32-36 inches
  • Diet: Fruits, nuts, seeds
  • Status: Least Concern

Iconic beak

Toucan

Famous for their large, colorful beaks, toucans are essential seed dispersers in tropical rainforests.

Key Facts:

  • Beak length: Up to 8 inches
  • Weight: 1-2 lbs
  • Diet: Fruits, insects
  • Status: Least Concern

Social birds

Cockatoo

Highly intelligent and social parrots with distinctive crests, known for their playful personalities and vocal abilities.

Key Facts:

  • Lifespan: 40-60 years
  • Intelligence: Very high
  • Diet: Seeds, fruits, nuts
  • Status: Various (some endangered)

Fastest fliers

Hummingbird

The smallest birds with the fastest wing beats, capable of hovering and flying backwards with incredible precision.

Key Facts:

  • Wing beats: 50-80 per second
  • Size: 2-8 inches
  • Diet: Nectar, insects
  • Status: Least Concern

Colorful small parrots

Parakeet

Small, colorful parrots with long tail feathers, known for their cheerful chirping and social nature.

Key Facts:

  • Length: 7-18 inches
  • Lifespan: 5-15 years
  • Diet: Seeds, fruits, vegetables
  • Status: Least Concern

Large tropical birds

Hornbill

Large birds with distinctive casques on their bills, important seed dispersers in tropical forests.

Key Facts:

  • Size: 16-63 inches
  • Casque: Unique head structure
  • Diet: Fruits, insects, small animals
  • Status: Various (some threatened)
